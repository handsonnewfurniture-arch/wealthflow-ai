## State-by-State Analysis

### Florida (Tax Lien State)
**Max Interest:** 18% annually
**Redemption Period:** 2 years
**Format:** Mostly online auctions
**Investor Grade:** A+

**Best Counties:**
1. **St Johns** - Jacksonville area, wealthy suburbs, top-tier market
2. **Collier** - Naples/Marco Island, ultra-wealthy, near-perfect redemption
3. **Lake** - The Villages retirement community, stable, low risk
4. **Sarasota** - Gulf Coast, strong tourism, stable market
5. **Polk** - Lakeland/Winter Haven, growing, affordable entry points

**Why Florida Dominates:**
- 18% statutory max interest (one of the highest)
- Online auction format = accessible from anywhere
- Strong property values and appreciation
- High redemption rates (95%+)
- Well-established auction processes
- Tropical climate attracts retirees = stable demand

---

### Iowa (Tax Lien State)
**Max Interest:** 24% annually (HIGHEST IN US)
**Redemption Period:** 18-24 months
**Format:** Mix of in-person and online
**Investor Grade:** A+

**Best Counties:**
1. **Polk** - Des Moines metro, strong economy, insurance hub
2. **Linn** - Cedar Rapids, manufacturing base, stable
3. **Dallas** - West Des Moines suburbs, rapid growth

**Why Iowa is Underrated:**
- **24% interest rate** - literally the highest in the country
- Strong Midwest economy (insurance, ag, manufacturing)
- High redemption rates
- Lower competition than coastal states
- Stable, affordable property values

**The Catch:**
- Many auctions are in-person (requires travel or bidding service)
- Colder climate = slower appreciation than Sun Belt
- Rural areas can be risky (avoid vacant land)

## Scoring Methodology

Each county is scored 0-100 based on:

### Interest Rate Potential (25 points)
- 24%: 25 pts (Iowa)
- 18%: 23 pts (FL, IL, NJ, OH)
- 16%: 20 pts (AZ)
- 10-25%: 18 pts (IN)
- 6-24%: 15 pts (MD)

### Redemption Rate (25 points)
- 95%+: 25 pts (wealthy suburbs, retirement areas)
- 85-94%: 20 pts (stable metros)
- 75-84%: 15 pts (growth markets)
- <75%: 10 pts (declining areas)

### Property Value Strength (20 points)
- Median $400k+: 20 pts
- Median $250k-400k: 15 pts
- Median $150k-250k: 10 pts
- Median <$150k: 5 pts

### Competition Level (15 points)
- Low (smaller counties, less known): 15 pts
- Medium (decent size, some competition): 10 pts
- High (major metros, institutional): 5 pts

### Auction Accessibility (15 points)
- Online: 15 pts
- Hybrid: 10 pts
- In-person only: 5 pts

### Crime/Risk Score (bonus/penalty)
- Low crime (<30): +5 bonus
- High crime (>60): -10 penalty

## Red Flag Counties to AVOID

### Avoid These Despite Good Interest Rates:
1. **Cook County, IL** - Chicago, too competitive, institutional dominance
2. **Wayne County, MI** - Detroit, declining values, low redemption
3. **All extremely rural counties** - Vacant land, no buyers, foreclosure risk
4. **High-crime urban cores** - Property values declining, foreclosure risk
5. **Flood zones** - Environmental risk, uninsurable
6. **Any county with redemption rate <70%** - Too likely to foreclose
